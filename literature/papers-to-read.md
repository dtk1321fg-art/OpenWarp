# Papers to Read

This is the starting literature map for OpenWarp. Each paper should eventually get its own one-page summary.

## Traversable Wormholes and Entanglement-Based Mechanisms

### Morris and Thorne — Wormholes in Spacetime and Their Use for Interstellar Travel

**Paper:** Wormholes in spacetime and their use for interstellar travel: A tool for teaching general relativity  
**Authors:** Michael S. Morris and Kip S. Thorne  
**Link:** https://pubs.aip.org/aapt/ajp/article-abstract/56/5/395/1037343/Wormholes-in-spacetime-and-their-use-for

Why it matters:

- Introduces the classic traversable wormhole framework.
- Defines the redshift and shape-function approach used in the current OpenWarp toy model.
- Makes the exotic-matter problem explicit.

Summary needed:

- What metric is proposed?
- What traversability constraints are required?
- Where does NEC violation appear?
- What assumptions are made about exotic matter?

### Gao, Jafferis, and Wall — Traversable Wormholes via a Double Trace Deformation

**Paper:** Traversable Wormholes via a Double Trace Deformation  
**Authors:** Ping Gao, Daniel Louis Jafferis, Aron C. Wall  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1608.05687

Why it matters:

- Shows a mechanism where coupling the two boundaries of an eternal black hole produces negative averaged null energy.
- Gives a serious entanglement / quantum-information route to traversability.
- Important because it does not look like a simple classical exotic-matter shell.

Summary needed:

- What is the double-trace deformation?
- What supplies the negative averaged null energy?
- Why does it not violate causality?
- How dependent is the result on AdS/CFT assumptions?

### Maldacena, Milekhin, and Popov — Traversable Wormholes in Four Dimensions

**Paper:** Traversable wormholes in four dimensions  
**Authors:** Juan Maldacena, Alexey Milekhin, Fedor Popov  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.04726

Why it matters:

- Gives a four-dimensional traversable wormhole construction.
- Uses charged massless fermions to create a negative Casimir-like energy.
- Directly relevant to OpenWarp's question of whether microscopic throats can be stabilized without macroscopic exotic matter.

Summary needed:

- What theory and fields are required?
- How does the negative Casimir-like energy appear?
- What size limits are required?
- Does it allow matter traversal or mainly information transfer?
- What makes it different from the Morris-Thorne toy model?

### Freivogel, Fumagalli, and Tomašević — How Traversable Is a Traversable Wormhole?

**Paper:** How traversable is a traversable wormhole?  
**Authors:** Ben Freivogel, Alessandro Fumagalli, Marija Tomašević  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12528

Why it matters:

- Studies low-frequency scattering in the Maldacena-Milekhin-Popov four-dimensional wormhole.
- Helps distinguish "the throat exists" from "useful signals or matter can actually pass through."
- Important for OpenWarp's information-transfer versus matter-transfer split.

Summary needed:

- Which probes traverse efficiently?
- Which probes are reflected or trapped?
- What does this imply for useful wormhole channels?
- Does this strengthen or weaken the OpenWarp network idea?

## Warp Metrics and Theory

### Alcubierre — The Warp Drive

**Paper:** The warp drive: hyper-fast travel within general relativity  
**Author:** Miguel Alcubierre  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/0009013

Why it matters:

- Introduced the famous warp-drive metric.
- Shows how spacetime could mathematically contract in front and expand behind.
- Creates the central exotic-matter / negative-energy problem.

Summary needed:

- What metric is proposed?
- What stress-energy is required?
- Where does negative energy appear?
- What assumptions are made?

## Quantum Inequality and Negative Energy Limits

### Pfenning and Ford — Quantum Inequality Restrictions

**Paper:** The unphysical nature of warp drive  
**Authors:** Michael J. Pfenning and L. H. Ford  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9702026

Why it matters:

- Analyzes whether quantum field theory can support the negative energy needed by warp drives.
- Argues that quantum inequality limits make standard warp-drive requirements extremely severe.

Summary needed:

- What are quantum inequalities?
- What limits do they impose?
- What does this imply for Alcubierre-style warp?

## Modern Warp-Drive Frameworks

### Bobrick and Martire — Physical Warp Drives

**Paper:** Introducing Physical Warp Drives  
**Authors:** Alexey Bobrick and Gianni Martire  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.06824

Why it matters:

- Reframes warp drives as a broader class of spacetime shells.
- Discusses positive-energy subluminal warp-drive configurations.
- Important for separating sublight warp concepts from FTL claims.

Summary needed:

- What do they define as a warp drive?
- What does positive-energy mean here?
- Does it solve FTL? If not, why not?

### Santiago, Schuster, and Visser — Generic Warp Drive Limitations

**Paper:** Generic warp drives violate the null energy condition  
**Authors:** Jessica Santiago, Sebastian Schuster, Matt Visser  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.03079

Why it matters:

- Critiques generic warp-drive models.
- Focuses on null energy condition violations.
- Helps test whether positive-energy claims survive stronger observer-frame analysis.

Summary needed:

- What is the null energy condition?
- What counts as a generic warp drive?
- Where does the violation appear?

## Negative Energy and Vacuum Physics

### Ford — Negative Energy Review

**Paper:** Negative Energy Densities in Quantum Field Theory  
**Author:** L. H. Ford  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/0911.3597

Why it matters:

- Reviews where negative energy appears in quantum field theory.
- Explains the difference between local negative energy and usable negative energy.
- Important for understanding the real experimental bottleneck.

Summary needed:

- What examples of negative energy exist?
- What limits their magnitude and duration?
- What could be experimentally relevant?

## Casimir and Vacuum Engineering

### Casimir Effect Background

**Topic:** Casimir effect and vacuum energy between boundaries  
**Starting point:** https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0204125

Why it matters:

- Casimir systems are one of the most realistic experimental paths connected to vacuum-energy effects.
- But Casimir energy is tiny and not automatically useful for warp drive.

Summary needed:

- What is actually measured?
- Is the energy density negative in the relevant region?
- How strong is the effect?
- Can geometry change it?

## Reading Method

For every paper, create a summary with:

1. Main claim
2. Required assumptions
3. Equations that matter
4. What evidence supports it
5. Weak points
6. Relevance to OpenWarp
7. What question it answers
8. What question remains open
