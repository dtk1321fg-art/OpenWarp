# OpenWarp Roadmap

This roadmap is organized as a sequence of scientific gates. Each gate must be treated skeptically. The project should only advance when evidence is strong enough.

## Phase 0 — Research Foundation

**Status:** active

Goal: build the public research map.

Deliverables:

- Define the project mission
- Collect the key papers
- Track no-go theorems
- Track possible loopholes
- Define what would count as proof
- Identify relevant labs and experimental fields

## Phase 1 — Existence

Goal: determine what forms of exotic or negative stress-energy are already accepted in physics, and which forms are only theoretical.

Core questions:

- What counts as exotic stress-energy?
- Which effects are experimentally measured?
- Which effects are only mathematical?
- What kind of stress-energy would warp metrics actually need?

Deliverable:

- Exotic Stress-Energy Evidence Map

## Phase 2 — Measurement

Goal: identify reproducible experimental systems that measure relevant vacuum or stress-energy effects.

Candidate lanes:

- Casimir systems
- Squeezed light
- Quantum optics
- Nanophotonics
- Precision interferometry
- Atomic or optical measurement systems

Deliverable:

- Measurement Methods Tracker

## Phase 3 — Control

Goal: find whether exotic stress-energy-like effects can be actively changed on demand.

Possible control knobs:

- Geometry
- Material choice
- Plate spacing
- Optical field state
- Cavity design
- Temperature
- Boundary conditions

Deliverable:

- Tunable Stress-Energy Candidate List

## Phase 4 — Shaping

Goal: investigate whether stress-energy can be arranged spatially into useful patterns.

Core question:

> Can a lab system produce an asymmetric or shell-like stress-energy distribution relevant to warp metrics?

Deliverable:

- Shaped Stress-Energy Design Notes

## Phase 5 — Scaling

Goal: determine whether the effect can grow or whether quantum inequalities and engineering limits make it impossible.

Core questions:

- Does scaling improve or destroy the effect?
- What are the energy requirements?
- Do quantum inequality limits dominate?
- Are there known materials or field states that improve scaling?

Deliverable:

- Scaling Laws Report

## Phase 6 — Spacetime Coupling

Goal: test whether engineered stress-energy creates measurable spacetime effects.

Possible signals:

- Phase shift
- Light path distortion
- Time delay
- Tiny gravitational field change
- Interferometer response

Deliverable:

- First Spacetime Coupling Experiment Proposal

## Phase 7 — Metric Engineering Prototype

Goal: design a tiny non-propulsive device that creates a controlled, measurable spacetime distortion.

This is not a spacecraft. It is a bench-scale proof of metric engineering.

Deliverable:

- Metric Engineering Prototype Concept

## Phase 8 — Propulsion Path

Goal: only after previous gates are passed, investigate whether metric engineering can become propulsion.

Required systems:

- Power source
- Field control
- Stability control
- Navigation
- Bubble formation and collapse safety
- Radiation and matter interaction models

Deliverable:

- Uncrewed Micro-Probe Concept
