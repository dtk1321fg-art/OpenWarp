# Memo Outline — What Actually Blocks Warp Drive?

## Working Title

**What Actually Blocks Warp Drive: Energy Conditions, Quantum Inequalities, and Vacuum Engineering**

## Purpose

This memo will become OpenWarp's first serious public research document.

It should answer:

> What would have to become experimentally real before warp-drive physics could move from speculation toward engineering?

## Thesis

Warp-drive physics is not mainly blocked by lack of imagination. It is blocked by the difficulty of producing the required stress-energy distribution.

The first real goal is not a spacecraft.

The first real goal is:

```math
T_{\mu\nu}^{engineered}(x,t)
```

A controllable engineered stress-energy tensor.

## Section 1 — What Warp Drive Means

Explain that warp drive is a spacetime geometry idea, not a conventional engine.

Include the Alcubierre metric:

```math
ds^2 = -dt^2 + [dx - v_s f(r_s)dt]^2 + dy^2 + dz^2
```

Main point:

> The ship is not locally moving faster than light; the surrounding spacetime geometry is changing.

## Section 2 — The Stress-Energy Requirement

Use Einstein's equation:

```math
G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi T_{\mu\nu}
```

Main point:

> If you want a geometry, you need the stress-energy tensor that creates it.

For Alcubierre, the energy density includes:

```math
\rho = -\frac{v_s^2}{32\pi}\frac{y^2+z^2}{r_s^2}\left(\frac{df}{dr_s}\right)^2
```

Main point:

> The original warp metric requires negative energy density in the wall region.

## Section 3 — Energy Conditions

Explain:

```math
T_{\mu\nu}u^\mu u^\nu \geq 0
```

and:

```math
T_{\mu\nu}k^\mu k^\nu \geq 0
```

Cover:

- Weak Energy Condition
- Null Energy Condition
- Why violations are serious
- Why quantum field theory complicates the story

## Section 4 — Quantum Inequality Limits

Explain the basic idea:

```math
\int \rho(t)W(t)dt \geq -\frac{K}{\tau^4}
```

Main point:

> Quantum theory may allow local negative energy, but it appears to restrict how much can exist and for how long.

## Section 5 — Existing Negative-Energy-Like Effects

Cover:

- Casimir effect
- Squeezed states
- Local negative energy in quantum field theory

Main point:

> Existing effects may prove that negative-energy-like physics is real, but not that useful warp-drive stress-energy is available.

## Section 6 — Hypotheses

Use the OpenWarp hypotheses:

1. Alcubierre-class bubbles require negative energy in the wall.
2. Negative energy alone is insufficient.
3. Quantum inequalities may be the main scaling barrier.
4. Casimir systems are evidence, not yet engineering.
5. Sublight metric engineering is a more plausible first milestone than FTL.

## Section 7 — First Real Experimental Target

Define the first target as tunable stress-energy:

```math
\frac{\partial T_{\mu\nu}^{lab}}{\partial u} \neq 0
```

Where `u` is a controllable lab parameter.

The first experiment does not need to make a warp bubble. It needs to show controlled stress-energy behavior relevant to the bottleneck.

## Section 8 — What Would Count as Progress?

Rank progress:

1. Literature map
2. Clean paper summaries
3. Evidence map
4. Tunable vacuum-effect proposal
5. Measurement method
6. Reproducible lab effect
7. Shaped stress-energy distribution
8. Measurable spacetime coupling
9. Metric-engineering prototype

## Section 9 — What Would Count as Failure?

A route should be considered weak if:

- it cannot define `T_{\mu\nu}`
- it ignores energy conditions
- it ignores quantum inequalities
- it cannot be measured
- it cannot be controlled
- it cannot scale
- it confuses analogue effects with actual spacetime curvature

## Final Claim

OpenWarp should not claim that warp drive exists.

OpenWarp should claim:

> The first serious path toward warp-drive physics is to investigate whether exotic stress-energy can be measured, controlled, shaped, scaled, and coupled to spacetime.
