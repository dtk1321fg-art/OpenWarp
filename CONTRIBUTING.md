# Contributing to OpenWarp

OpenWarp is a serious speculative-physics research project. Contributions should be skeptical, sourced, and honest about uncertainty.

## Contribution Types

You can contribute by adding:

- Paper summaries
- No-go theorem explanations
- Experimental method notes
- Research questions
- Literature references
- Measurement proposals
- Simulations, if clearly labeled as simulations
- Critiques of weak claims

## Rules

1. Do not claim warp drive is solved.
2. Do not present speculation as proof.
3. Link sources whenever possible.
4. Separate theory, experiment, and opinion.
5. Explain assumptions clearly.
6. Mark weak evidence as weak.
7. Respect no-go theorems and critiques.
8. Keep the project focused on peaceful research and human expansion.

## Paper Summary Format

Use this format:

```md
# Paper Title

## Source

Link:
Authors:
Year:

## Main Claim

## Key Assumptions

## Important Equations

## Evidence or Argument

## Weak Points

## Relevance to OpenWarp

## Questions Left Open
```

## Experiment Proposal Format

Use this format:

```md
# Experiment Title

## Claim Tested

## Setup

## Measurement Method

## Expected Signal

## False Positives

## Cost / Difficulty

## Required Expertise

## Relevance to OpenWarp
```

## Tone

The tone should be ambitious but skeptical.

Good:

> This paper may suggest a possible loophole, but the assumptions need to be checked against energy-condition constraints.

Bad:

> Warp drive is basically solved.
