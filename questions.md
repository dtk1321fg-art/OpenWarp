# Open Questions

This file tracks the questions OpenWarp must answer before any serious warp-drive claim can be made.

## Definition Questions

1. What exactly counts as exotic stress-energy?
2. What is the difference between negative energy density, exotic matter, and energy-condition violation?
3. Which forms are experimentally accepted?
4. Which forms are only theoretical?
5. What stress-energy distribution does an Alcubierre-like metric require?

## Physics Bottleneck Questions

1. Do all useful warp metrics require energy-condition violations?
2. Are there subluminal positive-energy warp configurations that survive stronger critiques?
3. Can quantum field theory provide enough negative energy for any useful metric effect?
4. Do quantum inequalities make scalable exotic stress-energy impossible?
5. Can modified gravity change the requirements without creating worse problems?

## Experimental Questions

1. Which lab systems are closest to stress-energy engineering?
2. Can Casimir systems be shaped beyond simple parallel-plate geometries?
3. Can squeezed states create measurable sub-vacuum effects relevant to stress-energy?
4. Can nanophotonics or metamaterials shape vacuum modes usefully?
5. What instrument could detect the smallest spacetime effect from a lab system?

## Scaling Questions

1. Does the effect scale linearly, nonlinearly, or collapse with size?
2. How much energy would be needed for a measurable spacetime signal?
3. What are the strongest known limits from quantum inequalities?
4. Can many small systems be combined into one larger effect?
5. Are there material limits that block scaling?

## Proof Questions

A claim should not be considered strong unless it answers:

1. What was measured?
2. What was the error margin?
3. Could another explanation fit the data?
4. Was it reproduced by another group?
5. Does it affect spacetime, or only electromagnetic fields?
6. Can it be controlled on demand?
7. Can it be shaped?
8. Can it scale?
