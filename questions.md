# Open Questions

This file tracks the questions OpenWarp must answer before any serious spacetime-engineering or effective-FTL claim can be made.

OpenWarp's current active route is **engineered traversable wormhole networks**: shorten the path instead of locally exceeding the speed of light.

## Definition Questions

1. What exactly counts as a traversable wormhole?
2. What is the difference between a geometric shortcut, an Einstein-Rosen bridge, a Morris-Thorne wormhole, and an entanglement-assisted traversable wormhole?
3. What exactly counts as exotic stress-energy?
4. What is the difference between negative energy density, exotic matter, NEC violation, and ANEC violation?
5. Which forms are experimentally accepted?
6. Which forms are only theoretical?
7. Which models permit matter traversal, and which only permit information transfer?

## Classical Wormhole Questions

1. What stress-energy distribution does a Morris-Thorne metric require?
2. Which choices of redshift function Φ(r) avoid horizons?
3. Which choices of shape function b(r) minimize exotic matter?
4. What tidal-force constraints appear for different throat sizes?
5. How does the negative mass-equivalent scale with throat radius?
6. Does any classical metric family reduce requirements below astrophysical scale?

## Quantum Inequality Questions

1. Can quantum field theory provide enough negative averaged null energy for any useful throat?
2. Do quantum inequalities make scalable wormhole stabilization impossible?
3. How does the QI gap scale with throat radius?
4. How does the QI gap scale with support time τ?
5. Does longer support time help, or does it tighten the bound?
6. Are there field states that change the scaling rather than only the coefficient?

## Semiclassical / Quantum Gravity Questions

1. Can quantum gravity permit Planck-scale connected throats?
2. Can entanglement generate or stabilize traversability?
3. What exactly does the Gao-Jafferis-Wall double-trace mechanism require?
4. What exactly does the Maldacena-Milekhin-Popov four-dimensional construction require?
5. Are these mechanisms limited to AdS/CFT, special black holes, special fermions, or extra-dimensional assumptions?
6. Can a microscopic throat be stable without becoming a black hole?
7. Does topology change require physics beyond known semiclassical gravity?

## Network Questions

1. Can tiny throats be chained into a useful shortcut?
2. Does chaining reduce the exotic-energy problem or multiply it?
3. Can many information-scale channels become a matter-scale route?
4. What synchronization problems appear between mouths?
5. What happens when one mouth is accelerated or moved over interstellar distances?
6. Can mouth motion create closed timelike curves or chronology-protection problems?

## Experimental Questions

1. Which lab systems are closest to stress-energy engineering?
2. Can Casimir systems be shaped beyond simple parallel-plate geometries?
3. Can squeezed states create measurable sub-vacuum effects relevant to stress-energy?
4. Can nanophotonics or metamaterials shape vacuum modes usefully?
5. Can analogue gravity systems test any part of the wormhole-network idea?
6. What instrument could detect the smallest spacetime-coupling signal from a lab system?
7. Is there any realistic observational signature of microscopic traversable throats?

## Scaling Questions

1. Does the required negative energy scale linearly, nonlinearly, or catastrophically?
2. How much energy would be needed for a measurable spacetime signal?
3. What are the strongest known limits from quantum inequalities?
4. Can many small systems be combined into one larger effect?
5. Are there material, quantum, or causality limits that block scaling?
6. Is a Planck-scale throat a meaningful engineering target or only a sign that the classical model has failed?

## Proof Questions

A claim should not be considered strong unless it answers:

1. What was calculated or measured?
2. What assumptions were used?
3. What was the error margin or approximation regime?
4. Could another explanation fit the data?
5. Was it reproduced by another group?
6. Does it affect actual spacetime, or only an analogue system?
7. Can it be controlled on demand?
8. Can it be shaped?
9. Can it scale?
10. Does it survive known no-go theorems?
